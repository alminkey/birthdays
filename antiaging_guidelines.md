## Executive Summary

This document compiles evidence-based anti-aging interventions across six key domains: Nutrition & Supplements, Physical Activity, Sleep & Recovery, Stress Management, Skincare, and Biomedical Interventions. Each recommendation includes evidence levels, actionable guidelines, and measurable metrics for mobile app integration.

**Evidence Level Classification:**
- **Level I**: Large randomized controlled trials (RCTs), meta-analyses, systematic reviews
- **Level II**: Smaller RCTs, cohort studies
- **Level III**: Case-control studies, observational studies
- **Level IV**: Expert consensus, preclinical evidence

## 1. Nutrition & Supplements

### 1.1 Anti-Aging Dietary Patterns

#### Mediterranean Diet
**Evidence Level: I** (Multiple RCTs, PREDIMED trial, meta-analyses)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- Prioritize olive oil as primary fat source (≥4 tbsp/day)
- Consume fatty fish 2-3 times per week
- Daily intake: vegetables (≥3 servings), fruits (≥2 servings), legumes (≥3 servings/week)
- Moderate wine consumption (optional): 1 glass/day for women, 2 for men
- Limit red meat to <2 servings/week

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Mediterranean Diet Adherence Score (0-14 scale)
- Target: Score ≥9 for optimal benefits
- Track: Weekly servings of key food groups

**Age-Specific Considerations (40-60):**
- Enhanced cardiovascular protection during perimenopausal/midlife transition
- Improved cognitive function maintenance observed in 50+ population
- Combined with intermittent fasting shows synergistic effects for this demographic

---

#### Caloric Restriction (CR)
**Evidence Level: II** (CALERIE trial, animal studies, ongoing human trials)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- Target 10-15% reduction from baseline caloric intake (not below 1,200 kcal women/1,500 kcal men)
- Emphasize nutrient density over calorie counting
- Monitor for unintended weight loss beyond 0.5 kg/week
- Ensure adequate protein (1.0-1.2 g/kg body weight)

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Caloric intake vs. estimated needs (target: 10-15% deficit)
- Body weight trajectory
- Resting metabolic rate (prevent >15% reduction)

**Age-Specific Considerations (40-60):**
- CR may help offset age-related metabolic slowdown
- Monitor bone density - may require calcium/Vitamin D supplementation
- Not recommended for individuals with history of eating disorders

---

#### Intermittent Fasting / Time-Restricted Eating (TRE)
**Evidence Level: II** (Multiple RCTs, 2024-2025 clinical trials)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- **Time-Restricted Eating (16:8)**: Fast for 16 hours, eat within 8-hour window
- Optimal eating window: 10:00 AM - 6:00 PM or 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM
- Begin with 12:12 fasting and gradually extend
- Stay hydrated during fasting periods (water, black coffee, plain tea allowed)
- Break fast with protein-rich meal

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Fasting window adherence (hours/day)
- Eating window consistency (same daily schedule)
- Glucose levels (fasting and postprandial)

**Age-Specific Considerations (40-60):**
- Improved insulin sensitivity particularly beneficial for this age group
- May improve sleep quality when eating window ends 3+ hours before bedtime
- TRE combined with Mediterranean diet shows enhanced benefits for older adults
- Morning-restricted eating (early time restriction) may align better with circadian rhythm

### 1.2 Key Supplements with Scientific Evidence

#### NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide) - NAD+ Precursor
**Evidence Level: II-III** (Multiple human clinical trials completed 2024-2025)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- **Dosage**: 250-500 mg/day (some protocols use up to 1,000 mg)
- **Timing**: Morning intake (mimics natural NAD+ circadian rhythm)
- **Duration**: Minimum 8-12 weeks to assess benefits
- Consider sublingual forms for enhanced bioavailability

**Measurable Metrics:**
- NAD+ blood levels (if available through testing)
- Insulin sensitivity markers
- Subjective energy levels (1-10 scale)
- Physical performance metrics

**Clinical Evidence Summary:**
- 2024-2025 clinical trials confirm safety and potential metabolic benefits
- Improved insulin sensitivity in overweight/obese adults
- Enhanced cardiovascular function markers
- NO longer considered "hype" - emerging as legitimate anti-aging intervention

**Age-Specific Considerations (40-60):**
- NAD+ levels decline ~50% by age 50; supplementation addresses this decline
- May improve age-related decline in mitochondrial function

---

#### Resveratrol
**Evidence Level: II-III** (Clinical trials with mixed results)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- **Dosage**: 150-500 mg/day (higher doses in some protocols up to 1,000 mg)
- **Timing**: With meal containing fat for absorption
- Often combined with NMN in longevity protocols
- Quality matters: look for trans-resveratrol ≥98% purity

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Inflammatory markers (CRP, IL-6)
- Lipid profile changes
- Insulin sensitivity markers

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Trials show modest improvements in insulin sensitivity and lipid profiles at 150-500 mg/day
- Synergistic effects with NMN suggested in preclinical studies
- Direct lifespan extension in humans not yet demonstrated

---

#### Metformin
**Evidence Level: I** (TAME trial ongoing, extensive observational data)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- **Dosage**: 500-1,500 mg/day (typically started at 500 mg)
- **Prescription Required**: Must consult physician
- Take with meals to reduce GI side effects
- Monitor B12 levels (metformin can deplete)

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Fasting glucose
- HbA1c
- Insulin sensitivity (HOMA-IR)
- Vitamin B12 levels

**Clinical Evidence:**
- TAME (Targeting Aging with Metformin) trial ongoing to prove anti-aging efficacy
- Observational studies suggest 20-30% reduction in all-cause mortality in diabetics
- Potential disease-preventing effects across multiple conditions

**Age-Specific Considerations (40-60):**
- Primary prevention benefits for cardiovascular disease
- May reduce cancer risk
- Contraindicated in significant kidney disease

---

#### Spermidine
**Evidence Level: II-III** (Human studies, strong preclinical evidence)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- **Dosage**: 1-3 mg/day from supplements, or through diet
- **Dietary sources**: Wheat germ, aged cheese, soy products, mushrooms
- Consider supplementation if dietary intake is low

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Autophagy markers (research settings)
- Hair spermidine levels (research)
- Cognitive function assessments

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Promotes autophagy (cellular cleanup mechanism)
- Associated with reduced cardiovascular mortality
- May support cognitive health

---

#### Omega-3 Fatty Acids (Fish Oil)
**Evidence Level: I** (Multiple large RCTs, meta-analyses)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- **Dosage**: 1-3 g combined EPA+DHA daily
- Look for high EPA formulations for anti-inflammatory effects
- Take with fat-containing meal
- Quality: Molecular distillation, third-party testing

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Omega-3 Index (target: 8-12%)
- Triglyceride levels
- Inflammatory markers (CRP)

---

#### Vitamin D3
**Evidence Level: I** (Extensive evidence for bone health, emerging for longevity)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- **Dosage**: 2,000-5,000 IU/day (adjust based on blood levels)
- Target blood level: 40-60 ng/mL (100-150 nmol/L)
- Take with fat-containing meal
- Combine with Vitamin K2 (100-200 mcg) for bone health

**Measurable Metrics:**
- 25-hydroxyvitamin D blood level
- PTH levels
- Bone density scans (DEXA)

---

#### Curcumin
**Evidence Level: II-III** (Clinical trials for inflammation, anti-aging mechanisms)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- **Dosage**: 500-1,000 mg/day (with piperine for 2000% increased absorption)
- Take with fatty meal
- Consider liposomal or phytosomal formulations

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Inflammatory markers (CRP, ESR)
- Joint pain/stiffness scores
- Oxidative stress markers

## 2. Physical Activity

### 2.1 Optimal Exercise Types for Longevity

#### Aerobic Exercise
**Evidence Level: I** (WHO guidelines, multiple meta-analyses)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- **Minimum**: 150 minutes moderate-intensity OR 75 minutes vigorous-intensity per week
- **Optimal for longevity**: 300+ minutes moderate OR 150+ minutes vigorous per week
- Include variety: walking, cycling, swimming, dancing
- Spread across at least 3-4 days per week

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Weekly aerobic minutes
- Heart rate during exercise (target zones)
- Steps per day (target: 7,000-10,000)
- VO2 max (if available)

---

#### High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)
**Evidence Level: II** (RCTs, systematic reviews)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- 1-2 HIIT sessions per week maximum
- Protocol example: 30 seconds work / 90 seconds active recovery × 10-15 cycles
- Total duration: 20-30 minutes including warm-up/cool-down
- Progress intensity gradually over 8-12 weeks
- Medical clearance recommended for high-risk individuals

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Peak power output
- Recovery heart rate (how quickly HR returns to baseline)
- VO2 max improvements

**Age-Specific Considerations (40-60):**
- Enhanced cardiovascular fitness and muscle strength benefits
- May improve cognitive function
- Proper recovery between sessions essential (48-72 hours)

---

#### Resistance Training / Strength Training
**Evidence Level: I** (Multiple RCTs, systematic reviews, WHO recommendations)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- **Frequency**: 2-3 sessions per week, non-consecutive days
- **Volume**: 8-10 exercises targeting major muscle groups
- **Sets/Reps**: 2-3 sets × 8-12 repetitions per exercise
- **Progression**: Progressive overload - increase weight/resistance 5-10% when 12 reps become easy
- Include compound movements: squats, deadlifts, presses, rows

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Strength gains (weight lifted for key exercises)
- Muscle mass (DXA or BIA)
- Grip strength (target: >26 kg women, >38 kg men)
- Chair stand test (repetitions in 30 seconds)

**Age-Specific Considerations (40-60):**
- **Critical for sarcopenia prevention** - muscle loss accelerates after 40
- 12 weeks of progressive strength training can reverse sarcopenia stage
- Maintain protein intake (1.0-1.2 g/kg) to support muscle synthesis
- Consider functional movements for daily activities

---

### 2.2 Recommended Frequency and Intensity (40-60 Age Group)

| Exercise Type | Frequency | Duration | Intensity | Notes |
|---------------|-----------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Aerobic (moderate) | 5x/week | 30-45 min | 50-70% max HR | Walking, cycling, swimming |
| Aerobic (vigorous) | 3x/week | 20-30 min | 70-85% max HR | Running, cycling, rowing |
| Resistance Training | 2-3x/week | 30-45 min | 60-80% 1RM | Non-consecutive days |
| HIIT | 1-2x/week | 20-30 min | 85-95% max HR | Allow 48-72h recovery |
| Flexibility/Mobility | Daily | 10-15 min | Light | Yoga, stretching |
| Balance Training | 2-3x/week | 10-15 min | Light | Tai chi, stability exercises |

### 2.3 VO2 Max Improvement Strategies

**Evidence Level: I** (VO2 max decline ≥4-5 mL·kg−1·min−1 per decade without intervention)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- HIIT most effective for VO2 max improvement
- Target VO2 max for age: 
  - Women 40-60: 28-35 mL·kg−1·min−1 (above average to excellent)
  - Men 40-60: 35-45 mL·kg−1·min−1 (above average to excellent)
- Zone 2 training (60-70% max HR) for 3-4 hours/week builds aerobic base
- Include one weekly VO2 max session (4-5 minutes at 90-95% max HR)

**Measurable Metrics:**
- VO2 max testing (lab or wearable estimation)
- Resting heart rate (lower = better fitness)
- Heart rate variability (HRV)
- Recovery time between intervals

---

### 2.4 Exercise Tracking Recommendations

**Minimum tracking for app integration:**
- Weekly exercise minutes (total and by type)
- Daily step count
- Strength training sessions/week
- Rest/recovery days
- Subjective exertion (RPE scale 1-10)

## 3. Sleep & Recovery

### 3.1 Optimal Sleep Duration and Quality Metrics

**Evidence Level: I** (Large cohort studies, meta-analyses)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- **Duration**: 7-9 hours for adults (consistent with successful aging studies)
- **Consistency**: Same bedtime and wake time ±30 minutes daily
- **Sleep efficiency**: Target >85% (time asleep / time in bed)
- **Sleep stages**: Adequate deep sleep (15-20%) and REM (20-25%)

**Measurable Metrics:**
| Metric | Target | Measurement Method |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| Total Sleep Time | 7-9 hours | Wearable/PSG |
| Sleep Efficiency | >85% | Wearable/PSG |
| Sleep Latency | <30 minutes | Wearable/PSG |
| WASO (Wake After Sleep Onset) | <30 minutes | Wearable/PSG |
| Deep Sleep % | 15-20% | Wearable/PSG |
| REM Sleep % | 20-25% | Wearable/PSG |
| Sleep Regularity Index | >80% | Sleep diary/wearable |

**Age-Specific Considerations (40-60):**
- Sleep architecture changes: less deep sleep, more awakenings
- May need slightly longer sleep to feel rested
- Address sleep disorders (apnea risk increases with age)

---

### 3.2 Circadian Rhythm Optimization

**Evidence Level: II** (Circadian rhythm research, intervention studies)

**Key Finding**: High-amplitude circadian rhythms correlate with wellbeing and increased lifespan

**Actionable Guidelines:**

**Light Exposure:**
- Morning bright light (10,000 lux) for 30 minutes within 1 hour of waking
- Midday outdoor exposure (maintain circadian amplitude)
- Evening light reduction: dim lights 2-3 hours before bed
- Blue light blocking after sunset or 2 hours before bed

**Meal Timing:**
- Consistent meal times daily
- Last meal 3+ hours before bedtime
- Consider time-restricted eating aligned with daylight

**Temperature:**
- Keep bedroom cool (65-68°F / 18-20°C)
- Body temperature naturally drops at sleep onset
- Warm bath/shower 1-2 hours before bed (triggers temperature drop)

**Social Rhythms:**
- Maintain regular social interactions
- Consistent daily routines reinforce circadian timing

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Morningness-Eveningness Questionnaire score
- Dim Light Melatonin Onset (DLMO) - research setting
- Activity rhythm amplitude (wearables)
- Social Rhythm Metric (SRM-5)

**Age-Specific Considerations (40-60):**
- Circadian amplitude naturally decreases with age
- Earlier chronotype shift common
- Enhanced circadian interventions can restore rhythmicity and promote healthspan

---

### 3.3 Sleep Tracking Biomarkers

**Digital/Wearable Biomarkers:**
- Heart rate during sleep
- Heart rate variability (HRV) - higher indicates better recovery
- Respiratory rate
- Body temperature
- Movement/restlessness
- Sleep stage distribution

**Physiological Biomarkers (clinical):**
- Melatonin levels (saliva or blood)
- Cortisol rhythm (salivary cortisol curve)
- Core body temperature rhythm

### 3.4 Recovery Protocols

**Daily Recovery Practices:**
- 7-9 hours quality sleep
- Proper hydration (30-35 mL/kg)
- Active recovery: light walking, stretching
- Stress management (see Section 4)

**Post-Exercise Recovery:**
- 48-72 hours between intense sessions for same muscle groups
- Protein intake within 2 hours post-exercise
- Sleep extension after intense training
- Contrast therapy (hot/cold) for muscle recovery

**Recovery Tracking:**
- HRV (higher = better recovery)
- Resting heart rate trends
- Subjective recovery scores (1-10)
- Performance metrics (stable or improving = adequate recovery)

## 4. Stress Management

### 4.1 Cortisol Management Techniques

**Evidence Level: I** (2024 meta-analysis of 58 clinical trials, 3,500+ participants)

**Key Finding**: Stress management interventions produce medium positive effect size (g = 0.282) on cortisol reduction

**Actionable Guidelines:**

**Most Effective Interventions (by effect size):**
1. **Mindfulness and relaxation practices** - highest cortisol reduction
2. Mind-body therapies
3. Talking therapies

**Daily Cortisol Management:**
- Morning: Bright light exposure, exercise, avoid checking stressful content
- Midday: Take breaks, mindful eating
- Evening: Wind-down routine, limit work stress, reduce screen time
- Night: Complete darkness, cool temperature for sleep

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Morning cortisol (saliva testing, 30 minutes after waking)
- Diurnal cortisol curve (saliva samples: waking, 30 min post, afternoon, evening)
- Hair cortisol (long-term cortisol exposure)
- Subjective stress ratings (PSS-10 or similar scale)

**Age-Specific Considerations (40-60):**
- HPA axis becomes less efficient with age
- May take longer to recover from stressors
- Chronic stress accelerates cellular aging

---

### 4.2 Mindfulness and Meditation Protocols

**Evidence Level: I-II** (Multiple RCTs, systematic reviews, meta-analyses)

**Key Finding**: Long-term meditation reduces stress and markers of aging; self-administered mindfulness significantly reduces short-term stress

**Actionable Guidelines:**

**Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR) Protocol:**
- **Duration**: 8-week program
- **Practice**: 45 minutes daily formal practice
- **Components**: Body scan, sitting meditation, mindful movement/yoga
- **Weekly**: 2-2.5 hour group sessions

**Daily Minimum Practice:**
- 10-20 minutes formal meditation
- Mindful moments throughout day (eating, walking)
- Consistent practice timing (morning preferred)

**Effective Techniques for Cortisol:**
1. Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction (MBSR)
2. Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT)
3. Guided meditations
4. Transcendental Meditation (TM) - long-term practice shows reduced aging markers
5. Breath-focused meditation (immediate cortisol reduction)

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Practice duration and frequency
- Mindful Attention Awareness Scale (MAAS)
- Perceived Stress Scale (PSS-10)
- Cortisol levels (before/after interventions)
- Heart Rate Variability (HRV)

**Long-Term Benefits:**
- 12-40 years of meditation practice: reduced gene expression changes associated with stress, improved cognitive function, lower hair cortisol

## 5. Skincare

### 5.1 Evidence-Based Anti-Aging Ingredients

#### Retinoids (Vitamin A Derivatives)
**Evidence Level: I** (Extensive clinical trials, systematic reviews 2015-2025)

**Actionable Guidelines:**

**Retinol (OTC):**
- **Concentration**: Start 0.25-0.3%, increase to 0.5-1% over time
- **Frequency**: Begin 2-3x/week, increase to nightly as tolerated
- **Application**: Evening only, pea-sized amount for face
- **Mandatory**: Sunscreen use next morning (retinoids increase photosensitivity)

**Tretinoin (Prescription):**
- **Strengths**: 0.025%, 0.05%, 0.1%
- **Most studied**: 0.05% tretinoin for photoaging
- Start low, increase strength gradually over 6-12 months

**Clinical Effects:**
- Reduces fine lines and wrinkles
- Improves skin texture and tone
- Reduces hyperpigmentation
- Increases collagen production
- Addresses both intrinsic and extrinsic aging

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Wrinkle severity scores (standardized scales)
- Skin roughness measurements
- Hyperpigmentation area/extent
- Skin hydration levels

**Age-Specific Considerations (40-60):**
- Skin becomes drier with age - pair with moisturizer
- May cause irritation - "sandwich technique" (moisturizer-retinoid-moisturizer)
- Results visible after 12-24 weeks of consistent use

---

#### Peptides
**Evidence Level: II** (Clinical trials, in vitro evidence)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- Look for: Matrixyl (Palmitoyl Pentapeptide-4), Copper Peptides, Argireline
- Can be combined with retinoids in routine
- Apply to clean skin before heavier products
- Morning and evening use safe

**Clinical Effects:**
- Signal collagen production
- Improve skin firmness and elasticity
- Some peptides provide Botox-like effects (Argireline)

---

#### Vitamin C (L-Ascorbic Acid)
**Evidence Level: II** (Clinical studies, dermatological consensus)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- **Concentration**: 10-20% L-ascorbic acid
- **Timing**: Morning application
- **pH**: Product should be pH 3.5 or lower for efficacy
- **Stability**: Look for opaque, airtight packaging
- **Synergy**: Use with Vitamin E and Ferulic acid for enhanced protection

**Clinical Effects:**
- Antioxidant protection against UV damage
- Brightening/hyperpigmentation reduction
- Collagen synthesis support

### 5.2 Sun Protection Protocols

**Evidence Level: I** (Highest impact on skin aging - "most crucial product for daily use")

**Actionable Guidelines:**

**Daily Sunscreen Use:**
- **SPF**: Minimum SPF 30, optimal SPF 50
- **Type**: Broad-spectrum (UVA + UVB)
- **Application**: 15 minutes before sun exposure
- **Amount**: 1/4 teaspoon for face, shot glass for body
- **Reapplication**: Every 2 hours, after swimming/sweating
- **Year-round**: Daily regardless of weather

**Physical/Mineral Sunscreens:**
- Zinc Oxide (20-25%) or Titanium Dioxide
- Better for sensitive skin
- Immediate protection

**Chemical Sunscreens:**
- Avobenzone, Octisalate, Octocrylene
- Lightweight, cosmetically elegant
- Apply 15 minutes before exposure

**Additional Protection:**
- Avoid peak sun hours (10 AM - 4 PM)
- Wear protective clothing, hats, sunglasses
- Seek shade when possible

**Measurable Metrics:**
- Daily sunscreen application (yes/no, frequency)
- SPF level used
- Reapplication frequency
- UV exposure time tracking

## 6. Biomedical Interventions (Longevity Medicine)

### 6.1 Key Biomarkers for Biological Age Assessment

**Evidence Level: II-III** (Emerging field, clinical validation ongoing)

#### Epigenetic Clocks

**Types:**
1. **Horvath Clock**: Multi-tissue epigenetic age predictor
2. **Hannum Clock**: Blood-based epigenetic age
3. **PhenoAge**: Phenotypic age predictor
4. **GrimAge**: Predicts mortality and lifespan (most validated for health outcomes)

**Actionable Guidelines:**
- Test epigenetic age annually
- Compare to chronological age (biological age < chronological age = optimal)
- Track changes over time in response to interventions

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Aging clocks outperform chronological age in predicting health outcomes
- Various clocks capture distinct aspects of aging
- Can predict healthspan and disease risks

---

#### Phenotypic Biological Age

**Blood-based Calculation:**
- Uses standard blood biomarkers
- Machine learning models estimate biological age
- Cost-effective and accessible

**Key Biomarkers for Phenotypic Age:**
- Albumin, Creatinine, Glucose, CRP
- Lymphocyte percentage, Mean cell volume
- Red cell distribution width, Alkaline phosphatase
- White blood cell count

### 6.3 Hormone Optimization Basics

**Evidence Level: II-III** (Varies by hormone)

**Key Principles:**
- Optimize, not maximize - target physiological youthful levels
- Address lifestyle factors first before supplementation
- Regular monitoring essential
- Work with qualified healthcare provider

#### Common Age-Related Hormone Changes (40-60)

**DHEA (Dehydroepiandrosterone):**
- Peaks at age 25, declines ~80% by age 70
- Target: Upper quartile of age-appropriate range
- Supplementation: 25-50 mg/day (women), 50-100 mg/day (men)
- Monitor: DHEA-S levels, side effects

**Testosterone (Men):**
- Declines ~1% per year after age 30
- Symptoms: fatigue, decreased muscle, low libido
- Testing: Total and free testosterone
- Replacement only if clinically indicated

**Estrogen/Progesterone (Women):**
- Perimenopause typically begins 40-50
- Hormone replacement therapy (HRT) decision: individual risk-benefit
- Window hypothesis: HRT benefits within 10 years of menopause onset

**Thyroid:**
- TSH tends to increase with age
- Target: mid-range or slightly lower
- Consider T3 optimization if symptoms persist

**Growth Hormone/IGF-1:**
- Declines with age
- Lifestyle optimization: sleep, exercise, protein intake
- GH therapy: controversial, mixed evidence for longevity

---

### 6.4 Emerging Therapies

#### Senolytics
**Evidence Level: III-IV** (Early human trials, strong preclinical evidence)

**Definition**: Drugs that selectively eliminate senescent ("zombie") cells

**Key Compounds:**

**Dasatinib + Quercetin (D+Q):**
- Most studied senolytic combination in humans
- Clinical trials in diabetic kidney disease, pulmonary fibrosis
- Protocol: Intermittent dosing (e.g., 3 days per month)
- Medical supervision required

**Fisetin:**
- Natural flavonoid
- Being studied in clinical trials
- Available as supplement (typically 500-1,000 mg)
- More research needed on optimal dosing

**Current Evidence:**
- D+Q shown to decrease senescent cells in humans
- Potential benefits for multiple age-related conditions
- Personalized approach recommended (ongoing research)

---

#### NAD+ Boosters
**Evidence Level: II** (Multiple human clinical trials completed)

**Types:**
- NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)
- NR (Nicotinamide Riboside)
- Niacin/Nicotinamide (less preferred)

**Clinical Evidence:**
- NMN clinical trials 2024-2025 confirm safety and metabolic benefits
- Increased NAD+ levels observed
- Improved insulin sensitivity
- Potential cardiovascular benefits

**Mayo Clinic Research Finding:**
- Combining NAD+ boosters with senolytics may hold key to longevity
- Restoring healthy cells (NAD+) + removing harmful cells (senolytics) = synergistic approach
